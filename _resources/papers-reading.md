How I keep up with the literature and what I hand to people who want to ramp
up on quantum information.

## Where to look

- [**arXiv quant-ph**](https://arxiv.org/list/quant-ph/recent) — The default.
  An RSS reader plus a few tags goes a long way.
- [**Quantum journal**](https://quantum-journal.org/) — Open-access, high
  quality, fast-ish review.
- [**Google Scholar alerts**](https://scholar.google.com/) — Set up alerts for
  authors and topics you track.
- [**Semantic Scholar**](https://www.semanticscholar.org/) — Useful for
  citation graphs and TLDRs.
- [**Connected Papers**](https://www.connectedpapers.com/) — Visualize a
  paper's neighborhood quickly.

## Textbooks I actually open

- **Nielsen & Chuang**, _Quantum Computation and Quantum Information_ — the
  standard reference; chapters 2, 4, 8, 10 in particular.
- **Wilde**, _Quantum Information Theory_ — best treatment of channel capacities
  and resource theories. [Free
  preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/1106.1445).
- **Watrous**, _The Theory of Quantum Information_ — rigorous, math-leaning.
  [Free PDF](https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/~watrous/TQI/).
- **Preskill**, [Caltech Ph 219 lecture notes](http://theory.caltech.edu/~preskill/ph229/)
  — still excellent.
- **Mermin**, _Quantum Computer Science_ — short, clear, great for new students.

## Survey starting points

- [**Quantum error correction (Roffe, 2019)**](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.11157)
- [**NISQ and beyond (Preskill, 2018)**](https://arxiv.org/abs/1801.00862)
- [**Variational quantum algorithms (Cerezo et al., 2021)**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.09265)
- [**Distributed quantum computing (Caleffi et al., 2022)**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10609)
